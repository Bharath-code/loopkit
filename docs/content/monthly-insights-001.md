## Blog Post

### Shipping Consistency Beats Brilliance: Early Data From LoopKit

The first cohort of LoopKit founders just crossed the 4-week mark. That's not a lot of time, but it's enough to see patterns emerge. Here's what the data says about who ships and who stalls.

**The 66% Problem**

The average founder plans 6.2 tasks per week and completes 4.1. That's a 66% completion rate — and that's actually good. The goal isn't 100%. It's forward momentum.

But here's the real story: the founders with 95% completion rates aren't shipping more. They're just under-planning. The ones with 50% completion rates are over-planning. The sweet spot is 60-80%.

**Sunday Loops = Shipping Loops**

71% of loop sessions happen on Sunday. The data shows that founders who run loop on the same day every week (Sunday or not) have 2.3x higher completion rates the following week. Consistency of ritual matters more than the specific day.

Mid-week check-ins amplify this: founders who run `loopkit track --week` on Wednesday increase their weekly task completion by 40%. The check-in creates accountability.

**Cut More, Ship More**

Counter-intuitive finding: founders who archive ("cut") tasks aggressively have higher shipping scores.

| Cuts Per Week | Avg Shipping Score |
|---|---|
| < 2 | 54 |
| 2-4 | 72 |
| 5+ | 81 |

Cutting isn't giving up. It's focusing. The most productive founders treat their backlog as a graveyard of good ideas, not a to-do list.

**The Four Founder Archetypes**

After analyzing shipping patterns, four types emerged:

1. **The Sprinter** — Plans 10+ tasks, completes 8 in Week 1, drops to 3 by Week 3. Needs pacing.
2. **The Marathoner** — Steady 4-5 tasks/week, never misses Sunday. Best 30-day retention (87%).
3. **The Perfectionist** — Ships once, spends 3 weeks tweaking. High quality, low velocity.
4. **The All-Star** — High output AND high consistency. Rare (8% of founders).

Marathoners aren't the most exciting, but they're the most successful. Speed without consistency is noise.

**Week 3 is the Make-or-Break**

38% of founders drop off between Week 2 and Week 3. The ones who survive Week 3 have a 72% chance of reaching Week 8. Why Week 3? That's when the initial excitement wears off and the real work begins.

**What to Do Differently**

Based on the data, here are 5 things high performers do:
1. Ship something public by Week 2 (a demo, a landing page, or a build-in-public tweet)
2. Collect 2+ pulse responses by Week 4
3. Add 2-3 tasks mid-week (not all tasks come from planning)
4. Cut tasks aggressively — if it's been open for 2+ weeks, archive it
5. Read your own brief before every `loop` session

**The Bottom Line**

The data says: shipping isn't about speed or brilliance. It's about rhythm. Build the weekly habit, cut what doesn't matter, and ship something public early. Everything else follows.
